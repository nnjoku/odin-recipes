Yam Porridge - Asaro


What is Yam Porridge?

The Nigerian Yam pottage or Yam Porridge or Asaro is a very delicious Yam recipe eaten by multiple tribes across Nigeria.

Ingredients

  • 1 medium Yam tuber
  • 1 Red Bell Pepper
  • 1 Large Tomato
  • 1 Habanero Pepper
  • 1 Large Onion divided into two
  • 3 cups of water
  • 0.5 cup Palm Oil
  • 2 tbsp crayfish
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 tsp chicken bouillon powder or two cubes
  • 15 ounces Mackerel fish or salmon, stockfish or dried fish
  • Basil or parsley or any leafy vegetable like spinach or kale

Instructions

  1. Peel and cut the Yam into medium-sized chunks. Rinse a couple of times until the water is no longer cloudy. Set aside.
  2. Blend the bell pepper, habanero, tomato, and half onion..
  3. Put the yams inside a pot, and add the blended sauce, salt, bouillon powder, and water.
    Cover and let it cook for 10 minutes on medium to high heat
  4. Stir in the palm oil, sliced onions, and crayfish, cover, and leave to cook for an additional 10 minutes.
  5. Mash the yams with a potato masher or a wooden spoon. I love to mash some yams and keep some chunks. It gives more texture!
  6. Add the fish. You can use whatever addition you prefer, like smoked fish or crab meat.
  7. Allow it to simmer for an additional 3 to 5 minutes.
  8. Stir in the herbs like basil, or leafy greens like spinach, kale and enjoy while still hot!
