Nigerian Kebab - Suya


What is Suya?

Suya is a delicious, spicy Nigerian grilled Meat delicacy. Skewers of thinly sliced meat, oven-grilled and doused with yaji spice.
There's nothing quite like suya. Its unique taste is irresistible! It's lip-smacking, finger-licking good!

Ingredients

  • 2.5 lb Beef Flank steak, skirt steak or Sirlion
  • 0.5 to 1 tsp salt
  • 0.5 tsp beef or chicken bouillon
  • Cayenne pepper - optional
  • 3/4 cup Suya or Yaji Spice plus little more for dusting
  • 1 tbsp peanut oil or any oil of choice

Instructions

  1. Prep the skewers: Soak the skewers in water for at least 30 minutes before using them.
  2. Prep the steak: Slice the meat into about 1/8-1/4 thin slices. Then cut into roughly 2-inch pieces.
  3. Season: Season the beef with salt and bouillon powder (or cube). Add the oil and mix well.
  4. Marinate: Cover with a tight-fitting lid or plastic wrap. Then leave it in the refrigerator for at least an hour to marinate.
  5. Thread the beef onto the skewersâ€”about 4 to 5 pieces per skewer.
  6. Coat with the suya spice - yaji.
  7. Cook at 400F for 20 minutes, flipping halfway through the cooking process.
  8. Serve: Remove from skewers and serve with freshly sliced tomatoes, onions, and cucumbers.
